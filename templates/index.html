    About

    Myself Sagar Kumar, and I'm currently immersed in an exciting academic project called "TrustTone: DeepFake Voice Detection System." I'm fortunate to have the guidance of Assistant Professor Dr. P. M. Kamble at the Department of Computer Science in Central University Karnataka. Together, we're diving deep into the world of detecting DeepFake voices, aiming to tackle the rising concerns about fake media. We're working hard to create a system that can reliably spot these manipulated voices, making sure people can trust the content they hear. It's a challenging but rewarding journey, and we're hopeful that our work will contribute to making the online world a safer place.

    Objective: TrustTone: DeepFake Voice Detection System

    TrustTone, as a deepfake voice detection system, likely aims to address the growing concerns surrounding the misuse of synthetic media. Here are three major objectives it might have:

    • Detection Accuracy: Develop highly accurate algorithms to reliably identify deepfake voices while minimizing false positives.
    • Real-time Detection: Develop the ability to instantly identify deepfake voices, enabling swift response and mitigation in live scenarios like voice calls or streaming, enhancing security against potential threats.li>
    • User Education and Awareness: Educate users and organizations about the risks of deepfake technology and promote the importance of verifying voice authenticity for enhanced security.

    TrustTone aims to bolster security and trust in voice-based interactions by focusing on accurate detection, seamless integration, user education, continuous improvement, real-time capabilities, and resilience against adversarial attacks. These objectives collectively strive to mitigate the risks posed by deepfake technology and foster a safer digital environment.
